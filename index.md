
## Preface — [Slack-Stack](https://ukb-dt.github.io/slack-stack/)

This document is not a theory of everything, nor a product specification, nor a moral treatise. It is a **working architecture**—an attempt to describe how a system might remain human under pressure.

The starting assumption is simple and unfashionable: most contemporary technological systems are not neutral tools that happen to cause harm, but **control systems optimized for speed, predictability, and extraction**. Addiction, over-engagement, and behavioral lock-in are not side effects; they are mathematically coherent outcomes of low-dimensional optimization.

The Slack-Stack begins from a refusal to accept that outcome as inevitable.

Rather than asking how to maximize engagement, compliance, or even well-being, it asks a different question:
**What must remain invariant for a system to avoid corrupting the humans who use it?**

The answer proposed here is structural, not ethical. Ethics can be optimized away. Architecture cannot—at least not without becoming something else entirely.

Throughout this text, a recurring pattern appears: a **pentadic structure** that distributes agency, time, value, and truth across non-commensurate roles. This is not symbolism. It is an engineering choice. Five dimensions introduce slack—degrees of freedom that prevent collapse into a single dominant feedback loop. Slack is not inefficiency; it is resilience.

The Slack-Stack treats the human not as a target to be predicted, but as a living system whose defining property is **irreducibility across time**. It privileges slow models over fast ones, integral memory over derivative reaction, exported value over captured attention. It assumes that any system worth building must be able to tolerate ambiguity, noise, and eventual irrelevance.

What follows is intentionally incomplete. Each section is framed as a constraint, a lens, or a warning rather than a prescription. The alphabetic structure is not a claim of totality, but a reminder that **closure is the enemy of slack**.

If this document succeeds, it will not persuade everyone. It will make certain kinds of systems impossible to justify—and certain others possible to imagine.

That is enough.

— *Slack-Stack*
